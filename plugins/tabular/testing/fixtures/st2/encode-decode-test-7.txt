["News from Heaven by Jennifer Haigh is a wonderfully crafted collection of interlocking short stories all centered around Bakerton, a small Pennsylvania coal mining town. The town is white, predominantly Polish and unsophisticated. Aside from the Baker mines there is no industry. It is a town of bars and churches.One story of two young women hired out of Bakerton to do housekeeping for Jewish families just before WWII is a classic of the impact of the big city on totally unsophisticated and innocent girls. The consequences reverberate in many of the stories taking place closer to the present in the 1970's and 80's. Another story relates love and companionship come unexpectedly in middle-age to a plain sort of woman of the town. A central theme is how this small town is dying as the coal gives out. As a consequence many of the young yearn to escape. Some do; most do not and even those who escape are forever tied to their past. One of the successful escapees visits and is humbled by the quiet dignity and bravery of his family who have stayed.Ms. Haigh has an unerring sense of the nobility of ordinary people. She captures their joys and suffering, aspirations and defeats with economy, grace and deep humanity. There are no monsters, no psychopaths, no tales of unbelievable cruelty. You can believe and empathize with each character. Their passions are real and human-sized. News from Heaven reminds me of Elizabeth Strout's Olive Kitteridge in the subtlety of its interlocking characters and in the quiet power of the writing. I cannot recommend this fine collection more highly."]
